Marie Curie

Physicist and Chemist

Marie Curie was a pioneering physicist and chemist who conducted pioneering research on radioactivity. She was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize and remains the only person to have won Nobel Prizes in two different scientific fields (Physics and Chemistry).

Born on November 7, 1867, in Warsaw, Poland, Marie Skłodowska Curie made groundbreaking contributions to the understanding of radioactivity and its properties. Her work laid the foundation for the development of X-ray machines.
